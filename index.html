About

Tracey Anarella

Documentary Filmmaker

Tracey Anarella is an award-winning filmmaker. Her debut film, "Jesse and the Fountain of Youth" (2013), was directed, shot, edited, and produced entirely by Ms. Anarella — establishing her as a true quadruple threat. It won the McMinnville Short Film Festival and earned nominations and awards from coast to coast.

Her second short, "Brooklyn United", screened at the Cannes Film Festival Short Corner and won the Platinum Remi Award at WorldFest Houston, earning sweeping international acclaim.

Her feature documentary "Not Black Enough" — starring Professor Henry Louis Gates (Harvard), actress Vanessa Williams, and Grammy-nominated Petey Pablo — has screened at 14+ major international film festivals and won Best Documentary in Dubai. Most recently, "Livingston Taylor – Life Is Good" won the Van Gogh Award at the Amsterdam International Film Festival.

One Nation
Divisible

A Film by Tracey Anarella

In a country built on the promise of unity, what forces are tearing us apart? One Nation Divisible is a fearless, intimate documentary that journeys across America to examine the deep political, racial, and cultural rifts fracturing communities — and the ordinary people on every side trying to bridge them.

With the same unflinching honesty and warmth that defined Not Black Enough, Tracey Anarella gives voice to Americans rarely heard, weaving their stories into a powerful portrait of a nation at a crossroads.
